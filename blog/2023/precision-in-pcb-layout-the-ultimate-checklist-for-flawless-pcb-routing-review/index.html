Introduction

In the intricate world of PCB (Printed Circuit Board) design, meticulous attention to detail is not just beneficial; it’s essential. The process of reviewing PCB routing is a critical step in ensuring the integrity and functionality of the final product. This article provides a detailed PCB routing review checklist tailored to guide engineers through this complex task. Drawing from extensive personal experience and expertise, the checklist is designed to be comprehensive, clear, and easily understandable, offering step-by-step guidance to optimize PCB routing.

PCB Routing Review Checklist

  1. Stack Layer Check: Reassess the stack layer configuration of the PCB and consult manufacturer recommendations if necessary. This step ensures optimal layer arrangement for your specific design.
  2. DRC (Design Rule Check) Compliance: Verify that your design adheres to the DRC and aligns with the manufacturing capabilities. This ensures that your PCB is manufacturable and meets industry standards.
  3. Component Placement According to Schematic Grouping: Place components on the PCB following the grouping established in the schematic. This organization facilitates a logical and efficient layout.
  4. Power Supply Design Review: Reevaluate the power supply design to ensure stability and efficiency. This check is crucial for the overall performance of the PCB.
  5. Decoupling Capacitors Positioning: Place decoupling capacitors close to the input pads for optimal performance. This minimizes noise and stabilizes the power supply to the ICs.
  6. ESD Protection for Inputs: Ensure inputs have ESD (Electrostatic Discharge) diodes and capacitors, followed by IC converters. This arrangement safeguards the components from static damage.
  7. Avoiding Signal Stubs in Connections: Ensure no signal stubs are introduced when connecting capacitors or other components, as they can cause signal integrity issues.
  8. Component Routing for Easy Soldering: Route components at 180 degrees for easier soldering. This orientation helps in reducing manufacturing errors.
  9. Ground Signal Via Placement: Place at least one or two vias near the pad for every ground signal to ensure a solid ground connection.
  10. Power Supply Converter IC Loop Attention: Pay special attention to the loop input and output for power supply converter ICs to ensure efficient power management.
  11. Ground Vias on IC Converter Loops: Add sufficient ground vias on loops of IC converters to maintain stable operation and heat dissipation.
  12. Trace Width for Ground-in Power Supply: Use a trace width of 0.4–0.6 for ground-in power supply designs for optimal performance.
  13. Via Dimension and Finishing: Pay attention to the dimensions and finishing of vias to ensure reliability and to prevent connection issues.
  14. Ground Via under Thermal Ground Plane IC: Place ground vias under the thermal ground plane of ICs to enhance heat dissipation.
  15. Test and Debug Point Addition: Add test points (0.9 mm) and debug points (0.6 mm) to facilitate testing and debugging of the PCB.
  16. Avoiding Signals under Top Layer Metal Components: Do not route signals under metal components on the top layer to prevent interference and signal integrity issues.
  17. Layer Numbering: Mark each layer with a number sign for easy identification during the manufacturing and assembly process.
  18. Signal Plane Integrity: Avoid breaking the signal plane, as this can lead to signal integrity issues.
  19. Via and Test Point Alignment: Align vias and test points with the trace to avoid introducing stubs, which can affect signal quality.
  20. High-Speed Design Check: Review the design for high-speed signal integrity, ensuring that all high-speed traces are optimized for performance.
  21. Impedance Matching Trace Width Calculation: Calculate the width of traces to match the impedance, particularly for high-speed and RF signals.
  22. Analog Signal review: Check analog signals for integrity and noise immunity.
  23. Ground Pour Between Analog and High-Speed Traces: Implement ground pours between analog and high-speed or differential traces to reduce interference.
  24. Differential and High-Speed Trace Protection: Ensure protection for differential, high-speed, and analog traces to maintain signal integrity.
  25. Signal Differential Pair Proximity: Keep differential pairs (like USB) close together to minimize cross-talk and interference.
  26. Annular Ring Check: Ensure annular rings are sized to accommodate imprecise and inaccurate drilling, enhancing the durability and reliability of vias.
  27. Tear Drop Implementation: Use tear drops on trace-to-via transitions if necessary to reduce stress and prevent trace lifting.
  28. Ground Array Placement in Empty Areas: Utilize empty areas on the PCB by placing ground arrays, enhancing the board’s overall stability and performance.

Conclusion

The PCB routing review checklist serves as a crucial tool for engineers, guiding them through the meticulous process of ensuring a well-designed and functional PCB. By adhering to this checklist, you can significantly enhance the quality and reliability of your PCB designs, leading to efficient manufacturing.
